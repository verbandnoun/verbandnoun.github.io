We noticed first time users were coming into the app but, they weren’t turning on their discount. This was one of our main KPIs. It was basically considered a first time conversion. We also looked at how many users kep their discount on past the first “market clear” which is just a term for end of auction.

These users varied from small mom and pop shops looking to accelerate early payment from customers to larger businesses where someone like an accounts payable clerk or comptroller maybe coming in to view what invoices were up for early payment.

Background & Context

More people needed to engage the early payment tools their first time in.

In unmoderated, recorded sessions we saw them come into their invoices and click around but, we coudn’t ask them about their behavior. They’d stay for some time in the app, but wouldn’t take meaningful action.

Ideating around invoices

solutions

Users seemed to be focused on their invoices, what if we were too?

We emailed newer first time users and ask them to give us 30 mins of their time to talk us through their first-time C2FO experience. 5 people were willing to talk to us about their experience.


The big questions we wanted answers to were why did they drop out of the app? And what were they doing with their invoices.


Our interview questions

• What does your company do?

• What is your role at the company?

• How did you hear about C2FO?

• Remembering back to your first time in C2FO, what stood out? Talk us through it.

The redesigned flow

Armed with the knowledge that they were unsure of who had their invoices and the fact that they spent time examining those invoices, I decided to redesign the onboarding flow around those invoices.

I decided to put the tutorial sequence in between the registration and the invoice selection.

First step after registration focused on their invoices and explained that they came from the customer (Costco) and that it was the amount available for early payment (so not all of their accounts payable with that customer).

Second step focused on the discounting aspect of the app. Here is where they set the discount that applies to their accounts payable on the previous step.

Third step spoke of the time to the end of the early payment auction. And that they needed to keep their discount active until after the market “call.”

I liken the redesign we implemented as similar to talking to someone who’s panicked that they found something where it shouldn’t be (or where they didn’t expect to find it). And with that curiosity they had when they saw their invocies, we took that as an opportunity to help teach them about how C2FO worked.
