Controlling a Problem versus Problem Immersion.

A theme I’ve noticed in technology development but, also in human behavior in general is that we like to check problems off, as though the act of checking off the problem or moving a user story to done has solved the problem.


What we don’t like at all, in any way shape or form, is to acknowledge a problem and exist within that problem.


I suspect this comes down to our wanting to control things. Think about it: when do we want to not control over our environment?

Summary

 

Subheading

I think we’re coming about a new age of problem solving. It involves immersion into a problem space: who’s having the problem, what kind of pain is it producing and how are they currently solving it.


A key rub of this, is that it requires people say they don’t know something. That they don’t have a clear answer to fill in the check box. This goes against a whole generation of labor. Once upong a time, 50 years ago, as a worker, you were once thought of as someone with specialization and you honed part of a tangible good to perfection, then sent it down the line. And it went in one direction: towards being finished.


In the 21st century, we must get comfortable with problems and admitting that we don’t know something when we don’t.


This requires leadership to embrace ambiguity with a clear goal, not an outcome. And outcome is when you tell a team how to solve a problem. A goal allows flexibility in how it’s solved.
