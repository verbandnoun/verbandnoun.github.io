This client wanted to expand and optimize a product that already existed as a tab within another system. At the time of this project, they were lazer-focused on their aftermarket strategy (basically, once a machine was sold). A key part of that strategy was improving the effectiveness of their dealerships in planning and acting on machine alerts.

These machine alerts acted as a service opportunity to the dealership.


The key business goals were…

• Allow machine monitoring specialists to find issues more quickly.

• Speed up time to machine pre-diagnosis.

• Consolidate the information going to service locations.

Expanding a Product

Background & Context

Improving Machine Monitoring for a Global Ag Manufacturer

Machine Monitoring Specialists monitor machines their dealership has sold. Their full time job was looking for critical alerts indicating machine issues. These issues can range from “operator is out of seat” to “fan belt is going to fail (catastrophic).”


Over a two week span, we interviewed Machine Monitoring Specialists.


• I was interested in knowing how they measured success along with how their organization measured their success.

• I had them take me through their day, from when they got into the office to when they started to plan for the day to how they dealt with putting out fires.

• Their key tasks in terms of frequeny and importance.
