Sprint had several customer service centers through the US. And with 50 million customers, they were extremely overworked. To reduce some of this burden, the business decided they wanted to help customers solve their own problems, instead of calling the Customer Service center.

Business challenge

Background & Context

The challenge was reduce some of the load off of customer service

Working with Customer Service, they provided us with a set of the top reasons customers called in. The list contained the following reasons.

• Make an appointment for service

• Find out how much data they had remaining

• Find out when their next bill was due


Our research team then took our questions into interviews and found out a couple things.

Improving Machine Monitoring for a Global Ag Manufacturer

The existing Sprint Zone app was crammed with too much unfocused content. It was thought of as more of a marketing tool.

The My Account page was unhelpful as it just routed users to content that couldn’t address all their needs.

We knew the current mobile app was not adequate for users to mainenance their accounts but, that didn’t mean we had a clear idea of the concept that would. So we decided that a problem statement would help us clarify our objectives in design and define principles that would help us generate ideas for solutions.

Rallying around a problem statement

Problem Statement

How might we allow users to service their account without having to call in to the customer service center.

  • Including checking their current bill and billing history.
  • Find out their current remaining data and data usage patterns.
  • Allow them to make an appointment with a service location.


A problem statement may seem inane but, it helps lock the team into a design hypothesis in a way. And from it we could build some design principles to abide by and use in critique.

Deciding design principles

Design Principles

  • No dead ends
  • Design for people on the go
  • Design for people that are frustrated
  • Use plain language
  • They just want to use the phone
  • Weigh privacy and achieving their goals
