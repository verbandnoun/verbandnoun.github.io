Recently a client brought me a wealth of feedback from our users. It was about 10 pages of issues with the tools they used that we had direct control over. Along with this feedback, they suggsted a wealth of solutions to fix the issues.


I guess I could have arrogantly said to myself that “users don’t know how to solve their own problems” but, I smiled and said, “Interesting. I’ll look them over. Thanks!”

Feedack Conundrum

 

Subheading

After perusing the feedback from our users, I came to a firm conclusion: This is a great list of possible stuff to improve but, it’s not integrated into what they’re actually doing in moment they’re doing it.


The reason that matters is, you want to catch their feedback in context, and I don’t just mean user interface when I say context. I mean a situation in real-time where someone is trying to get something done.


This is why usability testing around scenarios is so great. The scenario triggers the situation for the task that the user want’s to improve.

Lack of Integration

 

Subheading

Always tie feedback to a contextual moment. When a user says, “Hey, can you move that button one step back?” It’s a great idea to then ask them what that’s going to help them do and why does doing that matter so much to them. Then they’ll start telling you a story about a situation where it would help them.

What outcome are they driving towards in that moment? How does it intersect with the other things they’re trying to accomplish with your product?
