Trying to understand users is continually a feat. It’s relatively easy to understand what they’re doing. You ask them to take you through a key task they perform or a sequence they may undertake in their day to day. Or you ask them how they make decisions regarding a set of choices. You do that a few times and then you have a decent grounding for helping them with technology.


What I’ve found though is there is something curious that influences user behavior much of the time: the invisible things. You watch people do something, perform a task, etc. But it isn’t always clear why they’re doing it. What’s their motivation? What is the pain they’re alleviating? The invisible things are a lot of the time the culprit behind key observations. These can be things like habits or pressure from a cultural sense to succeed as an individual.


Take China for example. Sometimes I wonder why they’re so complicit when it comes to obeying their government. As an American, I know a good bunch of us don’t trust our government or at the very least think it should be much small, I digress. What I’ve found out about China recently is that it’s a very community-centered culture. Which means they think of their community first when dealing with something liek a pandemic.
